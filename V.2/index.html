Team Members

LiQuiD112

Steve "LiQuiD112" Arhancet is one of the co-oweners of Team Liquid. Steve's League of Legend's history starts with him playing the support position for Team Curse. He later became the team manager of Team Curse and head of the e-sports department of Curse Incorporated in 2011. Before the start of the 2015 LCS splilt, Steve had closed a deal rebranding Team Curse as the Team Liquid we know now. As of now Steve is the co-Owner of Team Liquid alongside Victor "Nazgul" Goossens, the original founder of Team Liquid.

DLim

David "DLim" Lim is the head coach of Team Liquid. David originally worked with Team Liquid's Academy team before being promoted to the main team in 2017. After a rough start to the 2017 split, David was replaced by a new coach and former player, "Cain".

Swaguhsaurus

Nick "Swaguhsaurus" Phan was the general manager of Team Liquid. He joined the team as general manager in 2017. However he was later replace due to the poor start that Team Liquid had. Nick now moves to Team Immortals as their new general manager. Team Liquid later announced Micheal Artress as the new general manager of Team Liquid.

Lourlo

Samson "Lourlo" Jackson started his League of Legends career in the North American League Challenger Series with Team Storm and later CLG Black (Counter Logic Gaming's academy team). In 2015 Lourlo was brought into Team Liquid to replace the previous Top Laner, Quas.

Reignover

Kim "Reignover" Ui-jin is one of the best jungers in the west. However, Reignover was not always the best. He started his career in Korea as one of the worst. In 2015 he was picked up by Fnatic Gaming (FNC) to play in the EULCS. Under FNC Reignover had won the both the spring and summer EULCS splits. He also became a World's semifinalist under FNC. For 2016, Reignover moved to the NALCS to play under Team Immortals who placed 3rd place for both splits. During the offseason Team Liquid and Team Immortals came to an agreement to trade jungers. Thus in 2017 Reignover became Team Liquid's starting jungler.

Goldenglue

Greyson "Goldenglue" Gilmer is somewhat of an infamous player of the North American League of Legends scene. Goldenglue had place in the NALCS and NACS for many splits now and have yet to find success. Goldenglue first joined Team Liquid as a mid laner for Team Liquid's academy team. Following the acquisition of Reignover, Goldenglue was promoted up to the main team.

Piglet

Chae "Piglet" Gwang-Jin started his career during the third season of professional League of Legends. Piglet started playing league of legends under SKT in Korea. Piglet alongside SKT took Korea and the world by storm, at the time SKT were the undisputed best team League of Legends has ever seen. Piglet and SKT would win their first world championship title during the Third Season League of Legends World Championship. Piglet was later dropped from SKT and picked up by Team Curse to become thier starting ad carry. Even after the brand change from Curse to Liquid, Piglet stayed with the team.

Matt

Matt "Matt" Elento is the support player for Team Liquid. Matt started his professional under Team Liquid's academy team. He would later be bumped up to the main roster due to his great play and management decision.

Team Curse

Before Team Liquid was Team Liquid they were known by the name of Team Curse. Aside from the team name, there was another curse that followed the organization: The Forever Fourth Meme Curse. Throughtout the team's history there was nothing other than 4th place finishes. Due to a number of reasons Owner Steve Arhancet rebranded as Team Liquid in 2015 and the new organization prepares in hopes to break the curse. Watch the video for info about the rebrading or read here.

Roster 2015

Making up Team Liquid's first roster was Quas, IWDominate, FeniX, Piglet, and Xpecial. Despite being a new team, the players are no strangers to the game. Quas and IWD both played for Team Curse, Xpecial has been a long time vertan of the game, FeniX had played in Korea under the name Miso for Jin Air, and Piglet is a Season 3 World Champion.

Spring Split 2015

Starting the spring Piglet was unable to play due to Visa issues, so TL fielded sub KEITHMCBRIEF. The team finished the first week 2-0 and looked strong even with a sub. With the return of Piglet the team was not doing too well causing TL to sub in Keith for two more weeks. After a short break Piglet returns and the team finishes the split tied at 9-9 and enters the Playoffs at 6th seed after a tie-breaker match with Team8. The team entered playoffs with a clean 3-0 vs CLG. In the semi-finals TL started 2-0 against C9 before getting reverse swept. Following the lost the team enters the 3rd place match against TIP. Liquid was then able to beat Impluse in an intense 5 game match for 3rd place.

Summer Split 2015

After having played a split together, TL gets ready to take on the Summer Split with their talented roster. Liquid dominated the summer split and ended the split with 13-5 record and a tie against CLG. After they defeated CLG for the 1st place bye they faced a renewed TSM in the semis. TL was forced to play for 3rd place once again after losing 3-1 to TSM. Once again TL faced TIP in a third place match, however this time TL were able to win the series 3-1. Despite another 3rd place finish the Team could not go to Worlds due to CLG beating TSM in the finals. Because of TSM's lost to CLG, TL had to play in the gauntlet for the last NA slot at worlds. TL faced a miracle C9 team in the gauntlet that had just won 2 reverse sweeps back to back. TL was only able to take one game off of C9 this series. C9 had beaten TL 3-1 and had earned the last slot to Worlds.

Ten Man Roster

During the offseason there was a plethora of roster changes for many teams, Liquid included. TL support Xpecial was the first to leave the roster and top laner Quas leave a few months later.Replacing the two would be ex-TDK support Smoothie and ex-CLGBlack top laner Lourlo. Also joining Team Liquid would be TL's sister team Liquid Academy. After the recent success of the Academy team, owner Liquid112 decided to field a 10-man roster consisting of the main team and the academy team.

Spring Split 2016

The first game of the 2016 split resulted in a Team Liquid lost. Shortly after the match TL Jungler IWDominate announced retirement and TLA Dardoch was sent to replace IWDominate for Day2. TLA support Matt also joined the main roster with Dardoch as the two are the shotcalling duo of the academy team. For the rest of the split TL made no substitutions and despite a rough early split had picked themselves up by the end of the split. The team finished in 4th place with 10-8 record. Liquid started the playoffs with a 3-0 win against 5th place NRG. The team would then lose a close 3-2 series against defending NALCS champions CLG (who are once again the NALCS champions). The team is once again fighting for 3rd place, and their opponent is the regular split 1st place Team Immortals. Liquid take a beating and loses 3-0 to Immortals.

Summer 2016

During the Summer Split of Season 6, internal problems led to changes among the team roster. Team Liquid Academy's Moon, replaced Dardoch in the jungler position for a short time. Shortly later Dardoch returned to the starting line-up. With Dardoch's return Piglet was demoted to Team Liquid's academy team essentially trading spots with Fabbbyyy the previous academy team's ad carry. Team Liquid would run the line up of Lourlo, Dardoch, Fenix, Fabbbyyy, and Matt until playoffs. Team Liquid finished the split in 5th/6th place after a lost to CLG in the quarterfinals. During this time Team Liquid also fielded two other players Arcsecond (jungler) and Jynthe (ad carry) to try and solve the interal roster problem.
